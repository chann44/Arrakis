## Design Context

### Users
Arrakis is used by engineering and security teams that self-host tooling and need fast, trustworthy visibility into software supply chain risk across repositories. They use the UI during routine monitoring and incident-driven triage, where speed and clarity matter.

### Brand Personality
Confident, vigilant, pragmatic. The interface should feel operational and decisive, not playful or decorative. It should communicate momentum and control while staying approachable for cross-functional teams.

### Aesthetic Direction
Industrial editorial command-center style: high-contrast hierarchy, warm signal colors, textured depth, and asymmetrical composition. Prefer a light default theme with tinted neutrals, strong focal moments, and restrained motion. Avoid generic cyber gradients, glassmorphism, and neon-on-dark patterns.

### Design Principles
1. Create one clear hero focal point per page that communicates the most critical operational signal.
2. Use dramatic hierarchy shifts (size, weight, contrast) to separate urgent from contextual information.
3. Keep interactions fast and legible; visual personality should never block core monitoring workflows.
4. Introduce texture and depth intentionally to reinforce trust and memorability without visual noise.
5. Maintain accessible contrast and reduced-motion support as non-negotiable quality bars.
